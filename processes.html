What is Die-casting?

Die casting is a manufacturing process that can produce geometrically complex metal parts through the use of reusable molds, called dies. The die casting process involves the use of a furnace, metal, die casting machine, and at least two dies.

Incoming

Raw materials, primarily aluminum alloys, are received and inspected for quality before being moved to the melting area.

Melting

The metal is melted in a furnace to a specific temperature, preparing it for the casting process.

Casting

Molten metal is injected into the dies under high pressure. It rapidly cools and solidifies into the final part shape.

Trimming

Excess metal and flashing are removed from the casted part to meet the final product specifications.

Design and Analysis

We utilize advanced 3D modeling software, including SolidWorks, for product design and development. This allows for precise design and simulation before manufacturing begins. For mold flow analysis, we use Adstefan, a specialized software that simulates the flow of molten metal into the die. This helps us predict and prevent potential defects, ensuring the quality of the final casted product. By using these powerful tools, we can optimize the design and manufacturing process, leading to higher quality products and more efficient production.
