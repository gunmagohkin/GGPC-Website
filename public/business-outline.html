What is Die-casting?

Die-casting is a manufacturing process in which a high-precision mould is installed into a die-casting machine, and molten metal (mainly Aluminum, Zinc, and Magnesium) is injected into the mould at high pressure and high speed.

Compared to Iron metals, Aluminum is lighter and can produce geometrically complex metal parts. Aluminum is used in vehicles such as automobiles and motorcycles, as well as in electrical and information equipment. Aluminum die-casting is friendly to the environment since the low density of alloys can reduce waste and scrap materials are recyclable.
